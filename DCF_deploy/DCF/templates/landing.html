// METHODOLOGY

How the DCF model works

01

Fetch Historical Free Cash Flow

We pull operating cash flow and capital expenditures from audited filings to derive the most recent Free Cash Flow — the starting point for all projections.

02

Project Future Cash Flows

Apply your custom growth rates year by year to extrapolate FCF over the projection horizon (1–10 years). Multi-stage growth is supported.

03

Calculate WACC via CAPM

Cost of equity = Risk-Free Rate + β × Market Risk Premium. Blended with after-tax cost of debt, weighted by capital structure, to arrive at WACC.

04

Compute Terminal Value

Using the Gordon Growth Model: TV = FCFn × (1 + g) / (WACC − g). This captures all value beyond the explicit forecast period.

05

Discount to Present & Derive Fair Value

Sum of present values of projected FCFs plus the discounted terminal value equals Enterprise Value. Subtract net debt, divide by shares outstanding.
